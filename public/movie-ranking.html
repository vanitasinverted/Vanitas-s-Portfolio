The Top 3

Neon Genesis Evangelion

Neon Genesis Evangelion is a groundbreaking anime series that debuted in 1995, created by Hideaki Anno and produced by Gainax. Set in a post-apocalyptic world, it follows Shinji Ikari, a reluctant teenage pilot of a giant biomechanical robot called an EVA, as he battles mysterious beings known as Angels. Beneath its mecha-action exterior, the series delves into deep psychological themes, existential philosophy, and human relationships, making it one of the most influential and thought-provoking anime of all time. Its complex narrative and ambiguous ending have sparked endless discussions and interpretations among fans

Death Note

Death Note is a psychological thriller anime and manga series created by Tsugumi Ohba and illustrated by Takeshi Obata. The story follows Light Yagami, a brilliant high school student who discovers a mysterious notebook that grants him the power to kill anyone by writing their name in it. As he takes on the identity of "Kira" and seeks to create a crime-free utopia, he becomes entangled in a high-stakes battle of wits with the enigmatic detective L. The series explores themes of morality, justice, and power, making it one of the most gripping and thought-provoking anime of its time.

Hunter X Hunter

Hunter x Hunter is a critically acclaimed anime and manga series created by Yoshihiro Togashi. It follows Gon Freecss, a young boy who embarks on a journey to become a Hunter—an elite adventurer with access to secret knowledge and vast opportunities—in hopes of finding his long-lost father. Along the way, he befriends Killua, Leorio, and Kurapika, each with their own goals and struggles. The series is known for its deep world-building, strategic battles, and complex characters, blending action with philosophical themes. With its unpredictable storytelling and emotional depth, Hunter x Hunter remains a standout in the shonen genre.
